Nine of Pentacles at a glance

Finbarre’s interpretation: The Nine of Pentacles represents self-sufficiency, cultivated pleasure and the right to enjoy what sustained effort has produced.

Uprightindependence, earned comfort, financial security, refinement, self-respect
Reversedfinancial dependence, deceptive appearances, isolation, bad faith, a project failing
Linked cardThe Empress - Both occupy abundant gardens, but the Nine emphasises personal attainment while The Empress represents generative abundance.

A. E. Waite's original description

A woman, with a bird upon her wrist, stands amidst a great abundance of grapevines in the garden of a manorial house. It is a wide domain, suggesting plenty in all things. Possibly it is her own possession and testifies to material well-being.

Waite's original divinatory meanings

Upright:

Prudence, safety, success, accomplishment, certitude, discernment.

Reversed:

Roguery, deception, voided project, bad faith.

Source: A. E. Waite, The Pictorial Key to the Tarot, first published in 1910, with illustrations by Pamela Colman Smith.

Waite's meaning in plain English

Waite's account is broader than the modern keywords and sometimes offers contradictory possibilities. Its practical contrast is between refinement and bad faith, with the surrounding cards deciding which historical attribution is most relevant.
